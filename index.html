Sardar Patel (1875 - 1950)

A Short Sardar Patel

  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat, India.
  • He belonged to a Leuva Patel family, part of the broader Patidar community.
  • He received his early education in local schools and later studied law in England.
  • After completing his law studies, he returned to India in 1913.
  • He established a successful law practice in Ahmedabad and was known for his expertise and integrity.
  • Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi, he joined the Indian independence movement.
  • He played a key role in the Kheda Satyagraha (1918) and the Bardoli Satyagraha (1928).
  • He earned the title "Sardar" (leader) for his leadership in the Bardoli struggle.
  • He became a close associate of Gandhi and an influential leader in the Indian National Congress.
  • He served as the President of the Indian National Congress in 1931.
  • He was actively involved in the Quit India Movement (1942).
  • He was instrumental in organizing the civil disobedience movements and was arrested several times for his activities against British rule.
  • He was appointed as the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India after independence.
  • He spearheaded the integration of over 500 princely states into the Indian Union.
  • He was known for his pragmatic and decisive approach in uniting India.
  • He established the All India Services, which helped create a stable administrative framework.
  • He is often referred to as the "Iron Man of India" for his steadfastness and strong will.
  • His vision and efforts laid the foundation for a unified and strong India.
  • He passed away on December 15, 1950, in Bombay (now Mumbai), Maharashtra, India.
  • He was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1991.
  • He is remembered with the Statue of Unity, the world's tallest statue, inaugurated in 2018 in his honor.
